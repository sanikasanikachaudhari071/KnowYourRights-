Harassment

▶ Article 14
Right to Equality: Ensures equality before the law and equal protection of laws for all individuals, which can be invoked to address instances of discriminatory harassment.
▶ Article 15:
Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth: Prohibits discrimination on various grounds including sex, which can be invoked to address cases of harassment based on gender or sex.
▶ Article 21:
Right to Life and Personal Liberty: Guarantees the right to life and personal liberty, which includes the right to live with dignity and free from harassment or intimidation.
▶ Article 22:
Protection against arrest and detention in certain cases: Provides safeguards against arbitrary arrest and detention, ensuring protection against harassment by state authorities.
▶ Article 23:
Prohibition of traffic in human beings and forced labor: Prohibits human trafficking and forced labor, addressing forms of exploitation and harassment.
▶ Article 39:
Certain principles of policy to be followed by the State: Directs the state to ensure that citizens, men and women equally, have the right to an adequate means of livelihood, which can indirectly address issues of economic harassment.
▶ The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013:
This Act defines sexual harassment and provides for the prevention and redressal of complaints of sexual harassment at the workplace. It mandates the establishment of Internal Complaints Committees (ICCs) in workplaces with 10 or more employees and prescribes penalties for non-compliance.
▶ The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005:
This Act aims to protect women from domestic violence and provides for civil remedies such as protection orders, residence orders, and maintenance orders. It defines various forms of domestic violence, including physical, sexual, emotional, verbal, and economic abuse
▶ The Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000
This Act contains provisions to address cyber harassment, including cyber stalking, cyberbullying, and online harassment. Sections such as 66E, 67A, and 67B deal with offenses related to privacy violations and the transmission of sexually explicit material online.
▶ Section 294 - Obscene acts and songs:
Explanation: Deals with the offense of indulging in obscene acts or singing obscene songs in public places, which may constitute a form of harassment.
▶ Section 354 - Assault or criminal force to woman with intent to outrage her modesty:
Explanation: Addresses the offense of assaulting or using criminal force against a woman with the intent to outrage her modesty, constituting a form of sexual harassment.
▶ Section 354A - Sexual harassment and punishment for sexual harassment:
Explanation: Specifically deals with the offense of sexual harassment, prescribing punishment for actions such as making unwelcome sexual advances, passing lewd remarks, or engaging in sexually colored remarks.
▶ Section 509 - Word, gesture, or act intended to insult the modesty of a woman:
Explanation: Penalizes words, gestures, or acts intended to insult the modesty of a woman, which may constitute a form of verbal or non-verbal harassment.
▶ Section 503 - Criminal intimidation:
Explanation: Deals with the offense of criminal intimidation, which may involve threats or intimidation aimed at causing fear or alarm, constituting a form of psychological harassment.
▶ Section 506 - Punishment for criminal intimidation:
Explanation: Prescribes punishment for criminal intimidation, which may include threats to harm or injure someone, constituting a form of harassment.
▶ Section 509 - Word, gesture, or act intended to insult the modesty of a woman:
Explanation: Penalizes words, gestures, or acts intended to insult the modesty of a woman, which may constitute a form of verbal or non-verbal harassment.
