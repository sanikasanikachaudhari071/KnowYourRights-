Domestics and Wildlife

▶ Article 48:
This article of the Directive Principles of State Policy states that it is the duty of the State to protect and improve the environment and to safeguard forests and wildlife in the country.
▶ Article 51A(g):
It is the fundamental duty of every citizen of India to protect and improve the natural environment, including forests, lakes, rivers, and wildlife, and to have compassion for living creatures.
▶ The Wildlife Protection Act, 1972:
This is the primary legislation for the protection and conservation of wildlife in India. It provides for the protection of certain species of animals, birds, and plants, and regulates hunting, trade, and transportation of wildlife and their derivatives.
▶ The Indian Forest Act, 1927:
While primarily focused on forests, this act also includes provisions for the protection of wildlife and their habitats, and the regulation of activities such as hunting and grazing in forest areas.
▶ The Forest Conservation Act, 1980:
Aimed at the conservation and management of forests, this act regulates the diversion of forest land for non-forest purposes and requires prior approval for such diversion from the central government.
▶ The Biological Diversity Act, 2002:
Provides for the conservation of biological diversity, sustainable use of its components, and fair and equitable sharing of benefits arising from the use of biological resources.
▶ The Environmental Protection Act, 1986:
While not specific to wildlife, this act provides for the protection and improvement of the environment, including measures to prevent pollution and conserve natural resources, which indirectly contribute to wildlife conservation.
▶ The Wildlife (Protection) Amendment Act, 2006:
Amends certain provisions of the Wildlife Protection Act, 1972, to enhance penalties for wildlife-related offenses and strengthen enforcement mechanisms.
▶ The Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006:
Recognizes the rights of forest-dwelling communities, including rights to use and protect forest resources, which have implications for wildlife conservation and management.
▶ Section 498A IPC:
This section deals with cruelty by husband or relatives of husband, where cruelty can be physical or mental harm caused to a woman with the intent to coerce her or her relatives to meet unlawful demands.
▶ Section 304B IPC:
This section deals with dowry death, where the death of a woman is caused by burns or bodily injury within seven years of marriage and it is shown that she was subjected to cruelty or harassment by her husband or his relatives in connection with demand for dowry
▶ Section 9:
prohibits hunting of specified animals and specifies penalties for violations
