Marriage & Divorce

▶ Article 14:
Ensures equality before the law and equal protection of the law, which is relevant in ensuring that victims of domestic violence have equal access to justice and protection under the law.
▶ Article 15:
Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth
▶ Article 21:
Guarantees the right to life and personal liberty, which includes the right to live a life free from violence and abuse, including domestic violence.
▶ Article 25:
Freedom of conscience and free profession, practice, and propagation of religion (pertaining to personal laws related to marriage and divorce among different religious communities)
▶ Article 51A(e):
Fundamental duties of citizens to promote harmony and the spirit of common brotherhood amongst all the people of India transcending religious, linguistic and regional or sectional diversities
▶ Article 44:
Uniform Civil Code (though not implemented yet, it aims for a common set of laws governing marriage, divorce, adoption, and inheritance for all citizens regardless of religion))
▶ Hindu Marriage Act, 1955:
Governs marriage among Hindus and provides provisions for registration of Hindu marriages, conditions for a valid Hindu marriage, restitution of conjugal rights, judicial separation, and divorce among Hindus..
▶ Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1937:
Applies to Muslims in India and governs matters such as marriage, divorce, maintenance, and succession according to Islamic law.
▶ Special Marriage Act, 1954:
Provides a special form of marriage for people of India and all Indian nationals in foreign countries, irrespective of the religion or faith followed by either party.
▶ Indian Christian Marriage Act, 1872:
Regulates marriage among Indian Christians and provides for the solemnization and registration of marriages among them.
▶ Indian Divorce Act, 1869:
Applies to Christians in India and provides provisions for divorce and judicial separation among Christians.
▶ Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act, 1936:
Governs marriage and divorce among Parsis in India.
▶ Dissolution of Muslim Marriages Act, 1939:
Provides grounds for Muslim women to seek dissolution of their marriages by a court.Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961: Prohibits the giving or taking of dowry in marriage.
▶ Section 498A - Husband or relative of husband of a woman subjecting her to cruelty
Addresses instances where a husband or his relatives subject a woman to cruelty, including physical or mental abuse, related to dowry demands.
▶ Section 306 - Abetment of suicide:
Pertains to instances where someone abets or encourages another person to commit suicide, which may have relevance in cases related to marital discord or harassment.
▶ Section 494 - Marrying again during lifetime of husband or wife:
Deals with the offense of bigamy, where a person marries again while their spouse is still alive and the previous marriage is legally valid.
▶ Section 497 - Adultery:
Addresses the offense of adultery, which involves voluntary sexual intercourse by a married person with someone other than their spouse.
▶ Section 498 - Enticing or taking away or detaining with criminal intent a married woman:
Deals with offenses related to enticing, taking away, or detaining a married woman with criminal intent, often referred to as abduction or kidnapping for marriage.
