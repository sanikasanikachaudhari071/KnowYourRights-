Education and Examination

▶ Article 21-A:
The Right to Education (RTE) Act, 2009, corresponds to this article, which mandates the State to provide free and compulsory education to all children aged 6 to 14 years.
▶ Article 45:
This article directs the State to endeavor to provide early childhood care and education for all children until they complete the age of six years.
▶ Article 41:
It mandates the State to provide within the limits of its economic capacity and development, for education and public assistance in case of unemployment, old age, sickness and disablement, and in other cases of undeserved want.
▶ Article 46:
Directs the State to promote with special care the educational and economic interests of the weaker sections of the people, and, in particular, of the Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes, and shall protect them from social injustice and all forms of exploitation.
▶ The Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009:
This act mandates free and compulsory education for children between the ages of 6 and 14. It lays down the norms and standards for schools and specifies the responsibilities of the government, local authorities, and schools.
▶ University Grants Commission (UGC) Act, 1956:
Governs higher education in India and provides for the coordination and determination of standards in universities. It also provides recognition to universities and disburses funds.
▶ Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) Act, 1962:
Establishes the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE), which conducts examinations for classes 10 and 12, and affiliates schools across the country.
▶ National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT) Act, 1961:
Provides for the establishment of the NCERT, which conducts research and develops educational materials and curriculum frameworks.
▶ National Testing Agency (NTA):
While not an act, the NTA is an autonomous organization established by the Government of India to conduct entrance examinations for higher educational institutions.
▶ Indian Institutes of Management Act, 2017:
Governs the Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) and provides for their establishment, incorporation, and functioning as institutions of national importance.
▶ State Education Acts:
Each state in India has its own education act that governs the administration and regulation of education within the state.
▶ Section 419 - Punishment for cheating by personation:
Deals with the offense of cheating by personation, which may occur in examinations where someone else takes the exam on behalf of another.
▶ Section 420 - Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property:
Addresses instances where individuals cheat by dishonestly inducing others to deliver property, which could include fraudulent activities during examinations or academic admissions.
▶ Section 420A - Fraudulently obtaining a degree from a university:
Specifically deals with the offense of fraudulently obtaining a degree from a university, which may involve misrepresentation or falsification of academic qualifications.
▶ Section 420B - False information to university, etc.:
Addresses the offense of providing false information to a university or educational institution, which may include submitting fake credentials or academic records.
▶ Section 465 - Punishment for forgery:
Prescribes punishment for forgery, which may apply to cases involving falsification of educational certificates, transcripts, or other academic records.
▶ Section 468 - Forgery for purpose of cheating:
Penalizes forgery committed with the intent to cheat, which could include forging examination hall tickets, answer sheets, or academic credentials.
▶ Section 471 - Using as genuine a forged document or electronic record:
Addresses the offense of using forged documents or electronic records as genuine, which may occur in the context of submitting fake academic credentials or certificates.
▶ Section 471A - Using as genuine a forged electronic record:
Similar to Section 471, deals with the offense of using forged electronic records as genuine, which may occur in cases of electronic fraud related to academic credentials or examination results.
▶ Section 473 - Making or possessing counterfeit seal, etc., with intent to commit forgery:
Penalizes the making or possession of counterfeit seals or stamps with the intent to commit forgery, which may be relevant in cases of forging academic documents or certificates.
