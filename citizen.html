Citizens

▶ Article 5:
Citizenship at the commencement of the Constitution. Every person who was at the commencement of the Constitution (26 January 1950) domiciled in the territory of India who was born in India.
▶ Article 6:
Rights of citizenship of certain persons who have migrated to India from Pakistan.
▶ Article 7:
Rights of citizenship of certain migrants to Pakistan.
▶ Article 8:
Rights of citizenship of certain persons of Indian origin residing outside India.
▶ Article 9:
Persons voluntarily acquiring citizenship of a foreign State not to be citizens.
▶ Article 10:
Continuance of the rights of citizenship Any person who is considered a citizen of India under any of the provisions of this Part shall continue to be citizens and will also be subject to any law made by the Parliament.
▶ Article 11:
Parliament to regulate the right of citizenship by law.
▶ Section 299 and 300 - Offenses Related to Causing Death:
Defines murder and culpable homicide, providing legal frameworks for prosecuting individuals who cause death unlawfully.
▶ Section 320 to 338 - Offenses Against the Human Body:
Covers offenses such as hurt, grievous hurt, and causing injuries, addressing harm inflicted on individuals' physical well-being.
▶ Section 354 to 376 - Offenses Related to Sexual Assault:
Addresses sexual assault, harassment, and violence against individuals, including citizens, protecting their bodily integrity and autonomy.
▶ Section 503 and 504 - Intentional Insult and Provocation:
Penalizes intentional insult or provocation intending to provoke a breach of the peace, safeguarding citizens' dignity and preventing public disturbances.
▶ Section 499 and 500 - Defamation:
Addresses defamation, protecting citizens' reputation and rights from false and damaging statements or publications.
