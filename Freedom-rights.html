Freedom Rights

▶ Article 19:
Protection of certain rights regarding freedom of speech.It refers to legal provisions or constitutional guarantees that safeguard specific aspects of freedom of expression from government interference or censorship.
▶ Article 20:
Protection in respect of conviction for offences. refers to legal safeguards and rights afforded to individuals to ensure that they are treated fairly and justly when accused or convicted of criminal offenses.
▶ Article 21:
Protection of life and personal liberty. This principle ensures that individuals have the right to live without fear of arbitrary deprivation of life or liberty by the state or any other entity.
▶ Article 21A:
Right to education. It refers that every individual has the right to access quality education without discrimination.
▶ Article 22.:
Protection against arrest and detention in certain cases.refers to legal safeguards and rights that limit the power of authorities to arrest and detain individuals without sufficient legal justification. These protections are often implemented to prevent arbitrary or unlawful arrests and detentions, ensuring that individuals' rights to liberty and due process are respected.
Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955:
This act was enacted to protect civil rights and prevent discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.
Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986:
This act prohibits the engagement of children in certain hazardous occupations and regulates the conditions of work for children in non-hazardous occupations.
Prevention of Atrocities (SC/ST) Act, 1989:
This act aims to prevent atrocities against members of Scheduled Castes (SCs) and Scheduled Tribes (STs) and provides for special courts for the trial of such offenses.
▶ Right to Information Act, 2005:
This act empowers citizens to seek information from public authorities to promote transparency and accountability in the functioning of the government.Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993: This act establishes the National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) and State Human Rights Commissions (SHRCs) to safeguard human rights and investigate violations thereof.
National Food Security Act, 2013:
This act aims to provide food and nutritional security by ensuring access to adequate quantities of quality food at affordable prices.
Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015:
This act provides for the care, protection, and rehabilitation of children in conflict with law or in need of care and protection.
Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Act, 2019:
This act recognizes the rights of transgender persons and prohibits discrimination against them.
