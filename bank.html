Banking

▶ Article 243H:
Provides for the powers of the RBI as India's central bank.
▶ Article 243W:
Establishes the State Bank of India as the central bank of the country.
▶ Article 246:
Enumerates the legislative powers of the Parliament and the State Legislatures with respect to various subjects, including banking and money.
▶ Article 282:
Empowers the Union and States to make grants for any public purpose.
▶ Article 292:
Deals with borrowing by the Government of India.
▶ Banking Regulation Act, 1949:
Governs the operation and regulation of banks in India, including provisions related to licensing, management, and supervision.
▶ Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881:
Regulates negotiable instruments such as promissory notes, bills of exchange, and cheques, which are fundamental to banking transactions.
▶ Securitization and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest (SARFAESI) Act, 2002:
Provides a legal framework for asset reconstruction companies and empowers banks to enforce security interests in case of loan defaults.
▶ Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999:
Regulates foreign exchange transactions and activities, including foreign investments and remittances.
▶ Section 415: Cheating :
This section defines cheating as fraudulently inducing another person to deliver any property or valuable security or to do or omit to do anything, causing damage or harm to that person. In simpler terms, it's about deceiving someone to gain an advantage, which results in harm to the deceived person.
▶ Section 416: Cheating by personation:
This section deals with cheating by pretending to be someone else or by knowingly substituting one person for another. It covers cases where a person impersonates someone else to deceive another person, leading to wrongful gain or loss.
▶ Section 420: Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property:
his section addresses cheating and dishonestly inducing the delivery of property. It punishes those who cheat and induce others to deliver property by fraudulent means, promising something of value in return, but failing to deliver on that promise.
▶ Section 467: Forgery of valuable security, will, etc.:
This section deals with forgery of documents such as valuable securities (like bonds or stocks), wills, or authorities to transact financial matters. It covers instances where someone falsifies or alters such documents with the intent to deceive or defraud others.
