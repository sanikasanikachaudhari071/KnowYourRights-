Agricultural

▶ Article 39(b) and (c):
Directs the State to ensure that the ownership and control of material resources are distributed to best serve the common good and prevent the concentration of wealth and means of production to the common detriment.
▶ Article 47:
Mandates the State to raise the level of nutrition and the standard of living, focusing on improving public health and agricultural production.
▶ Article 243G:
Empowers Panchayats to prepare plans for economic development and social justice, including those related to agriculture and animal husbandry.
▶ Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC) Acts:
Governed by state-specific laws, these Acts regulate the establishment and functioning of APMCs, ensuring fair trade practices and market access for agricultural produce.
▶ Essential Commodities Act, 1955:
Provides the government with powers to control the production, supply, distribution, and trade of essential commodities, including agricultural produce, to maintain their availability and prices.
▶ Farmers' Produce Trade and Commerce (Promotion and Facilitation) Act, 2020:
Aims to create an ecosystem allowing farmers and traders the freedom to sell and purchase agricultural produce outside of APMC markets, promoting competitive pricing and market access.
▶ Farmers (Empowerment and Protection) Agreement on Price Assurance and Farm Services Act, 2020:
Establishes a framework for contract farming agreements between farmers and sponsors, empowering farmers to engage with agri-business firms for farm services and sale of produce with price assurance.
▶ Land Reforms Acts:
Enacted by various states, these Acts regulate land ownership, tenancy, and land ceiling to ensure equitable distribution of land resources and promote agricultural productivity.
