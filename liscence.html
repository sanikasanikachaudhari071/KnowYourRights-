Liscence

▶ Article 19(1)(g):
Provides citizens with the right to practice any profession, or to carry on any occupation, trade, or business, subject to reasonable restrictions imposed by the State in the interest of the general public.
▶ Article 19(6):
Allows the State to impose reasonable restrictions on the exercise of the right conferred by Article 19(1)(g) in the interest of the general public.
▶ The Motor Vehicles Act, 1988:
Regulates the licensing of motor vehicles and drivers, prescribing the qualifications and conditions for obtaining various types of driving licenses.
▶ The Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940:
Governs the licensing and regulation of the manufacture, sale, and distribution of drugs and cosmetics in India.
▶ The Shops and Establishments Act:
Enacted by states, these acts regulate the licensing and working conditions of shops, commercial establishments, and other businesses.
▶ The Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006:
Regulates the licensing and standards for food businesses to ensure the safety and quality of food products.
▶ The Indian Telegraph Act, 1885:
Regulates the licensing and operation of telegraph services and telecommunications networks in India.
▶ The Petroleum Act, 1934:
Governs the licensing and regulation of the storage, transport, and distribution of petroleum and petroleum products.
▶ The Arms Act, 1959:
Regulates the licensing and possession of firearms and ammunition in India.
▶ The Copyright Act, 1957:
Provides for the licensing and protection of copyright in literary, artistic, and musical works.
▶ The Trade Marks Act, 1999:
Regulates the licensing and registration of trademarks to protect intellectual property rights.
▶ The Patent Act, 1970:
Governs the licensing and protection of patents to encourage innovation and technological advancement.
▶ Section 420 - Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property:
Addresses instances where individuals cheat by dishonestly inducing others to deliver property, which could include fraudulent activities related to licensing or permits.
▶ Section 463 - Forgery:
Prescribes punishment for forgery, which may apply to cases involving falsification of licenses, permits, or other official documents.
▶ Section 465 - Punishment for forgery:
Similar to Section 463, this section deals with the offense of forgery and prescribes punishment for the same.
▶ Section 471 - Using as genuine a forged document or electronic record:
Addresses the offense of using forged documents or electronic records as genuine, which may occur in cases of presenting fake licenses or permits.
▶ Section 482 - Punishment for using a false property mark:
Deals with the offense of using a false property mark, which could include falsifying licensing or certification marks.
▶ Section 483 - Counterfeiting a property mark used by another:
Penalizes counterfeiting a property mark used by another, which may include copying or imitating licensing marks or symbols.
▶ Section 485 - Making or possessing counterfeit seal, etc., with intent to commit forgery:
Addresses the offense of making or possessing counterfeit seals or stamps with the intent to commit forgery, which may be relevant in cases of licensing fraud.
