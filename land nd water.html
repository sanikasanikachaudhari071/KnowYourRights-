Liscence

▶ Article 39(b) and (c):
Directs the State to ensure that the ownership and control of material resources are distributed to best serve the common good and prevent the concentration of wealth and means of production to the common detriment.
▶ Article 47:
Mandates the State to raise the level of nutrition and the standard of living, focusing on improving public health and agricultural production.
▶ Article 48A:
It is the obligation of the State to protect and improve the environment and to safeguard forests and wildlife in the country.
▶ Article 51A(g):
It is the fundamental duty of every citizen of India to protect and improve the natural environment, including forests, lakes, rivers, and wildlife, and to have compassion for living creatures.
▶ Article 243ZD - 243ZG:
Empowers Panchayats and Municipalities to prepare plans for economic development and social justice, including those related to land use, water supply, and environmental conservation.
▶ Land Ceiling Acts:
Enacted by various states, these laws prevent the concentration of land ownership and facilitate the redistribution of surplus land to landless farmers.
▶ Tenancy Acts:
Govern the relationship between landlords and tenants, ensuring fair rent, security of tenure, and protection against eviction.
▶ Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006:
Recognizes the rights of forest-dwelling communities, including rights to cultivate land, collect forest produce, and manage forest resources.
▶ Land Revenue Acts:
Govern land administration, land records, and revenue collection, often including provisions for land distribution and settlement of land disputes.
▶ Inter-State River Water Disputes Act, 1956:
Provides for the adjudication of disputes relating to the use, distribution, and control of waters of inter-state rivers and river valleys.
▶ National Water Policy, 2012:
Provides guidelines for the equitable and sustainable development and management of water resources, addressing water allocation, pricing, and conservation.
▶ State Water Acts:
Regulate water allocation, distribution, and management within the state, complementing national policies and addressing local water challenges.
▶ Water Users' Associations (WUAs):
Voluntary associations formed by water users to manage and distribute water for irrigation purposes, operating under state government guidelines.
▶ Water Supply and Sanitation Acts:
Regulate the provision of safe drinking water and sanitation services, including water distribution systems and wastewater treatment.
▶ Section 426 - Punishment for mischief:
Addresses the offense of mischief, which may include damaging or tampering with land or water-related infrastructure, such as dams, levees, or irrigation channels.
▶ Section 429 - Mischief by killing or maiming cattle, etc., of any value or any animal of the value of fifty rupees:
Addresses the offense of mischief by killing or maiming animals, which may include livestock grazing on land or near water bodies.
▶ Section 430 - Mischief by injury to works of irrigation or by wrongfully diverting water:
Deals with the offense of causing mischief by damaging or wrongfully diverting water from irrigation systems, which could affect agricultural activities or public water supply.
▶ Section 431 - Mischief by injury to public road, bridge, river or channel:
Addresses the offense of causing mischief by damaging public roads, bridges, rivers, or channels, which are essential for transportation, irrigation, or water management.
▶ Section 435 - Mischief by fire or explosive substance with intent to cause damage:
Deals with the offense of causing mischief by fire or explosive substance with the intent to cause damage, which could include arson or sabotage affecting land or water resources.
▶ section 437 - Punishment for mischief by destroying or moving, etc., a landmark fixed by public authority:
Penalizes mischief by destroying or moving landmarks fixed by public authority, which could include boundary markers or demarcations related to land or water bodies.
▶ Section 438 - Punishment for mischief by causing inundation or obstruction to public drainage attended with damage:
Deals with the offense of causing inundation or obstruction to public drainage systems, which could lead to flooding or waterlogging, causing damage to property or public health.
▶ Section 447 - Punishment for criminal trespass:
Penalizes unauthorized entry onto land or water bodies, aiming to protect property rights and prevent unlawful occupation or use.
▶ Section 448 - Punishment for house-trespass:
Addresses the offense of house-trespass, which may involve unauthorized entry onto land or property, including structures or dwellings near water bodies.
▶ Section 451 - House-trespass in order to commit offense punishable with imprisonment:
Deals with the offense of trespassing into a building or structure with the intent to commit an offense punishable with imprisonment, which could include offenses related to land or water resources.
▶ Section 457 - Lurking house-trespass or house-breaking by night in order to commit offense punishable with imprisonment:
Addresses the offense of lurking house-trespass or house-breaking by night with the intent to commit an offense punishable with imprisonment, which could involve unlawful activities affecting land or water resources.
