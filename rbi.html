RBI(Reserve Bank of India)

▶ Article 112:
This article deals with the presentation of the annual financial statement (Budget) of the Union government before Parliament, which includes the financial estimates of the RBI.
▶ Article 202:
This article states that no demand for a grant shall be made except on the recommendation of the President, based on the estimates presented in the annual financial statement, which includes the RBI's financial estimates.
▶ Article 204:
This article deals with the appropriation of moneys out of the Consolidated Fund of India to meet the expenditure charged on it, including payments related to the RBI's functioning.
▶ Article 282:
This article allows both the Union and State governments to make grants for any public purpose, which may include grants to the RBI for specific purposes.
▶ Article 283:
This article deals with custody of the Consolidated Fund of India and Contingency Fund of India, which may include funds managed by the RBI on behalf of the government.
▶ Article 284:
This article allows the President to make rules for the more convenient transaction of the business of the government relating to the amounts charged on the Consolidated Fund of India, which may involve transactions with the RBI.
▶ The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 (PWDVA):
This is the primary legislation aimed at protecting women from domestic violence. It defines domestic violence broadly to include physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse. It provides for protection orders, residence orders, and monetary relief to victims.
▶ The Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961:
Prohibits the giving or receiving of dowry, which is often associated with domestic violence against women. The Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 2013: While not specific to domestic violence, this act amended various provisions of the Indian Penal Code, the Code of Criminal Procedure, and the Indian Evidence Act to enhance punishment for sexual offenses and provide better protection to women against violence.
▶ The National Commission for Women Act, 1990:
Establishes the National Commission for Women (NCW), which is responsible for investigating and examining all matters relating to the safeguards provided for women under the Constitution and other laws.
▶ The Domestic Violence Rules, 2006:
These rules provide details regarding the implementation of the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, including the procedure for filing complaints, the duties of protection officers, and the conduct of proceedings.
▶ Section 323 - Voluntarily causing hurt:
Penalises the act of voluntarily causing hurt to another person, which includes physical violence causing bodily harm.
▶ Section 324 - Voluntarily causing hurt by dangerous weapons or means:
Addresses cases where hurt is caused by dangerous weapons or means, imposing a higher penalty for more severe forms of violence.
▶ Section 325 - Punishment for voluntarily causing grievous hurt:
Provides punishment for voluntarily causing grievous hurt, which refers to causing severe bodily injury or harm to another person
▶ Section 498A - Husband or relative of husband of a woman subjecting her to cruelty:
Specifically deals with cruelty by husbands or relatives towards married women, encompassing physical, mental, or emotional abuse.
▶ Section 375 - Rape:
Defines the offense of rape, which involves non-consensual sexual intercourse with a person without their consent.
▶ Section 376 - Punishment for rape:
Prescribes punishment for the crime of rape, with penalties varying based on the circumstances and severity of the offense.
▶ Section 376A - Punishment for causing death or resulting in a persistent vegetative state of victim:
Addresses cases where rape results in the death of the victim or causes them to enter a persistent vegetative state.
▶ Section 376AB - Punishment for rape on woman under twelve years of age:
Imposes stricter punishment for the rape of a girl under the age of twelve, reflecting the gravity of the offense against a minor.
▶ Section 504 - Intentional insult with intent to provoke breach of the peace:
Penalises intentional insults aimed at provoking a breach of the peace, often associated with verbal abuse or humiliation.
▶ Section 509 - Word, gesture or act intended to insult the modesty of a woman:
Addresses acts intended to insult the modesty of a woman through words, gestures, or actions, which may include sexual harassment or lewd behavior.
