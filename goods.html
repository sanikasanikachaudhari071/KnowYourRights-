Goods & Shipping

▶ Article 19(1)(f):
Right to acquire, hold, and dispose of property, which includes the right to engage in construction and redevelopment activities.
▶ Article 21:
Right to Life and Personal Liberty, which includes the right to shelter. This article is often invoked in cases related to housing and redevelopment projects.
▶ Article 47:
Duty of the State to raise the level of nutrition and the standard of living and to improve public health. This article can be relevant in matters related to housing and construction of habitable spaces.
▶ Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA):
Governs the real estate sector in India, including construction, redevelopment, and sale of properties. It aims to promote transparency, accountability, and efficiency in the real estate sector.
▶ Town and Country Planning Acts:
Various states in India have their own town and country planning acts which regulate land use, development, and redevelopment within their jurisdictions.
▶ Building Bye-Laws:
Each state and local authority has its own set of building bye-laws which provide guidelines for construction activities including building codes, safety norms, setback requirements, etc.
▶ Land Acquisition Act, 2013:
Governs the acquisition of land for public and private purposes, including for construction and redevelopment projects.
▶ Environmental Laws:
Acts such as the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, and the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981, among others, regulate environmental aspects of construction and redevelopment projects to ensure sustainable development and environmental protection.
▶ Section 268 - Public nuisance:
Explanation: Addresses acts which cause common injury, danger, or annoyance to the public or people in general, including those arising from construction activities impacting public safety or health.
▶ Section 427 - Mischief causing damage to the amount of fifty rupees:
Deals with the offense of mischief causing damage to property, which may include damage caused during construction or redevelopment activities.
▶ Section 447 - Punishment for criminal trespass:
Explanation: Penalizes unauthorized entry onto property with the intent to commit an offense, which can occur during construction or redevelopment without proper authorization.
